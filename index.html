Section 2

Issues & positions

A divorce trial is decided issue by issue. List each issue, what you're asking for, what the other side likely wants, and what you'll need to show.

What "burden" means. For most issues, whoever asks the court to do something has to give the court enough evidence to support it. Think about what facts the judge would need to be convinced of, and what proves each one. The exact standard varies by state — note your state's rule in the last column.

Section 10 · Recognition practice

Court vocabulary — recognize terms and common pitfalls

Become familiar with words you may hear in court and the common misunderstandings behind them. The goal is recognition: knowing what a term generally refers to, noticing when you may need clarification, and avoiding assumptions. It is not an objection script or case-specific advice.

Recognition practice only. These cards help users recognize unfamiliar vocabulary and common traps—for example, assuming every out-of-court statement is automatically excluded or assuming a document is usable merely because it exists. The court and governing rules determine what applies. Use the cards to create questions for official court resources or an attorney, not as instructions for what to say.

Important Use Notice

This product is a general court-preparation and document-organization template. It helps users arrange information they choose to enter, including dates, documents, possible exhibits, people, deadlines, questions, hearing notes, and parenting schedules.

What this template does not do

  • It does not provide legal advice, legal representation, legal document preparation, or an attorney-client relationship.
  • It does not choose claims, defenses, arguments, witnesses, questions, objections, deadlines, or courtroom strategy.
  • It does not determine whether information is accurate, relevant, authentic, admissible, sufficient, complete, compliant, or likely to produce a particular outcome.
  • Its checklist and dashboard report only what has or has not been entered or marked inside the organizer.
  • The vocabulary cards are for familiarity and recognition only. They do not tell a user when a term applies or what to say in court.
  • The parenting calendar does not create, interpret, or modify a custody order. A signed order or valid written agreement controls.

User responsibility

Court rules, forms, deadlines, evidence requirements, parenting provisions, and procedures vary by jurisdiction, court, case type, judge, and individual order. Users are responsible for checking official court sources and obtaining advice from a licensed attorney when needed.

Privacy and files

Entries are stored locally in the user’s browser and device unless the user independently exports, prints, shares, or transfers them. The seller does not receive, review, verify, or monitor information entered into this offline file.

This organizer and its exports are not intended to be filed with a court unless the user independently confirms that a specific document is accepted and appropriate for filing.
